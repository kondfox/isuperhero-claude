# iSuperhero — Full Game Rules

Source: https://www.isuperhero.ru/rules (translated from Russian)

## Objective

Tame three monsters by placing them in monster beds on your ship. To do this, develop your SuperAbilities and become a true SuperHero.

## Players

2–4 players (each with their own superhero passport).

## Setup

### Materials Needed
- Multiple A4 paper sheets
- Colored pencils (minimum 6 colors)
- Markers (one per player)
- 20-sided die
- SuperHero Equipment cards
- SuperHero passport (one per player)
- Cosmos deck (shuffled, placed face-down in center)

### The Passport

Each player's passport tracks five **SuperAbilities**, each rated on a 0–5 scale:

| # | SuperAbility | Domain |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Management | Executive function, planning, self-control |
| 2 | Communication | Speech, social interaction, expression |
| 3 | Orientation | Spatial awareness, left-right, navigation |
| 4 | Processing | Attention, memory, pattern recognition |
| 5 | Movement-Energy | Motor coordination, physical activity, energy |

### Difficulty Level

Each player selects based on their age:
- **Level 1**: Ages 5–7 (easiest tasks)
- **Level 2**: Ages 7–12 (medium tasks)
- **Level 3**: Ages 12+ (hardest tasks)

Players of different ages can play together, each at their own difficulty.

### Starting Player

Determined by whoever answers a prompt the fastest (e.g., "What did you eat for breakfast?", "What's your current mood?", "What did you do last weekend?").

## Turn Structure

On your turn, you perform **one or two actions**:

### Action 1: Develop a SuperAbility

1. **Choose** one of the five SuperAbilities to develop
2. **Roll** the 20-sided die → the result is your task number
3. **Complete** the corresponding task from the instruction manual for that ability (at your difficulty level)
4. **On success**: mark +1 on that ability's scale AND +1 on one related ability scale (two abilities improve per successful task)
5. **If the task is a repeat**: you may re-roll
6. **If struggling**: difficulty can be adjusted down

### Action 2: Draw from the Cosmos Deck

Draw the top card. Three possible outcomes:

#### Outcome A — Bonus Card
- Place beside your passport
- Can be used **once** at any time during the game
- Provides a special one-time advantage

#### Outcome B — SuperGame Card
- A memory-based mini-game triggers:
  1. Reveal Cosmos cards one by one until a **Monster card** appears
  2. All players study the monster for **10 seconds**
  3. Flip the monster face-down
  4. First player to correctly identify the **monster's outline** (silhouette) claims it
- This is a competition among all players — not just the active player

#### Outcome C — Monster Card (Battle!)
- A direct battle with the drawn monster
- Compare **each** of your five ability scores against the monster's five corresponding scores
- **Victory condition**: Your score must be **strictly higher** than the monster's score on **ALL five abilities**
- If ANY ability is equal to or lower than the monster's, you lose the battle

## Battle Outcomes

### Victory
1. Claim the monster card
2. Draw/illustrate the monster in one of the three empty monster beds on your ship
3. You are one step closer to winning

### Defeat — Choose One
**Option A**: Lose 1 earned point from any SuperAbility of your choice

**Option B**: Reflect on the monster's behavior, identify how it connects to your own life/behavior, and explain the connection aloud. If the explanation is accepted, you avoid the penalty entirely. (This is the therapeutic/reflective element of the game.)

## Bonus Cards

- Earned by drawing from the Cosmos deck
- Each card has a unique one-time effect
- Can be played at any point during the game (even during another player's turn, depending on the card)
- Exact bonus effects are defined on each card

## Winning the Game

The **first player to tame three monsters** (fill all three monster beds on their ship) wins.

## Session Management

Games can span multiple sessions:
- Preserve all passport ability markings
- Track collected monsters
- Track earned bonus cards
- Resume from the exact state later

## Key Design Principles (for digital adaptation)

1. **Tasks are real-world activities** — many require physical movement, drawing, speaking aloud, or using props. The digital version must account for this (e.g., timer + self-report, camera verification, or adapted digital equivalents).
2. **Mixed-age play** — different difficulty levels at the same table. The digital version must support per-player difficulty.
3. **SuperGame is multiplayer-reactive** — all players compete simultaneously in the memory mini-game, not just the active player.
4. **Reflection mechanic** — the "explain the connection" defeat option is narrative/therapeutic. Digital version needs a way to handle subjective responses (e.g., text input + peer/host vote, or AI evaluation).
5. **Bonus cards can interrupt** — some may be playable on other players' turns, requiring real-time notification.
6. **Flexible pacing** — the physical game has no timer pressure (except the 10-second monster memory). Digital version should preserve this relaxed pace.
